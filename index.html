Abstract

Machine translation (MT) performance has been improved significantly in the past few years Within the MT and NLP communities, English is by far the most resource-rich language. MT systems are most often trained to translate texts from and to English, or they use English as a source or target language to translate low-resource languages. However, MT coverage for more users speaking diverse languages is limited by the fact the MT methods demand huge amounts of parallel data to train quality systems, which has posed a major obstacle for low resource translation. Therefore, developing MT systems with relatively small parallel datasets is still highly desirable in this shared task for the English-Assamese language pair. The main challenge here is how to efficiently utilize monolingual data or techniques such as multilingual, transfer learning to improve translation performance for English-to-Assamese and Assamese-to-English. Evaluation will be carried out using automatic evaluation metrics (BLEU, TER, RIBES) and human evaluation.
